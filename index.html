What is HRV?
Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is the variation in time between heartbeats and is a key indicator of stress and recovery.
How to Interpret Stress Levels
Low HRV often indicates stress, while higher HRV suggests a more relaxed state. Consistency and trends are more important than individual scores.
What Welltory Measures & Why It Works
Welltory measures your Heart Rate Variability (HRV), not just your heart rate. HRV is the difference in time intervals between heartbeats, providing insights into your autonomic nervous system. It is a validated, non-invasive method used in cardiology, mental health, athletic performance, and cognitive research.

HRV shows how well your body copes with stress. Developed in the 1960s, HRV has been referenced in over 25,000 research papers. For example:
  • HRV-guided training improved athletic performance (Kiviniemi et al., 2007).
  • Lower HRV increases post-heart-attack mortality risk (Buccelletti et al., 2009).
  • Depression symptoms correlate with lower HRV (Agelink et al., 2002).
Welltory’s algorithm uses over 12 million measurements and 5,000+ health assessments to deliver personalized insights. It provides contextual biofeedback — simple scores for stress, energy, and health — based on daily habits and physiological data.

HRV can be measured accurately using your phone’s camera or smartwatch, making it an accessible yet powerful wellness tool.
